Research Journey: Expectations vs Reality

1

What We Expected

Advanced RL algorithms would demonstrate superior performance over classical controllers and approach human-level capabilities in autonomous landing tasks. We anticipated that multi-constraint scenarios would challenge but not catastrophically fail RL systems.

2

What We Discovered

Reality revealed stark limitations: 0% RL success under maximum constraints vs 61% human success. PPO-Curriculum achieved only 23% success rate with 38.75× worse fuel efficiency than humans. Universal failure across all algorithms under realistic conditions.

3

Critical Insights

Current RL approaches exhibit rigid, memorized patterns rather than adaptive intelligence. SHAP analysis revealed correct feature focus (altitude: 11.4±2.1 importance) but lack of causal reasoning. Agents demonstrate reactive rather than anticipatory control strategies.

4

What This Means

The substantial human-AI performance gap demands fundamental algorithmic improvements before safety-critical deployment. Hybrid human-AI systems and graduated deployment frameworks are essential. Current explainability tools reveal feature usage but not reasoning processes.

5

Future Directions

Research must prioritize meta-learning for rapid adaptation, physics-informed causal reasoning, and human-in-the-loop operational frameworks. The three-tier deployment strategy (Classical→RL→Human) offers a practical pathway for gradual autonomy integration.
